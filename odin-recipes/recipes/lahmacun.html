Lahmacun

Lahmacun, also known as Turkish pizza, is a thin and crispy flatbread topped with a flavorful mixture of minced lamb or beef, onions, tomatoes, and aromatic herbs and spices. It offers a delightful explosion of savory and tangy flavors, perfect for rolling up with fresh vegetables and sauces. This popular street food captures the essence of Turkish cuisine and is a delicious and satisfying option for a quick meal or snack.

Ingredients for the dough

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup warm water

Ingredients for the topping

  • ½ pound ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Ingredients for serving

  • Fresh parsley
  • Lemon wedges
  • Lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers (optional)
  • Tahini sauce or garlic sauce (optional)

How to cook?

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, yeast, salt, and sugar. Make a well in the center and add the olive oil and warm water. Mix until a dough forms.
  2. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours until it doubles in size.
  3. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  4. In the meantime, prepare the topping by combining the ground lamb or beef, chopped onion, tomatoes, tomato paste, parsley, minced garlic, cumin, paprika, cinnamon, salt, and pepper in a mixing bowl. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  5. Divide the dough into smaller portions and roll each portion into thin rounds, about 1/8 inch thick. Place the dough rounds onto the prepared baking sheets.
  6. Spread a thin layer of the meat topping mixture onto each dough round, leaving a small border around the edges.
  7. Bake the lahmacun in the preheated oven for about 8-10 minutes or until the edges are crispy and the meat is cooked through.
  8. Once done, remove the lahmacun from the oven and let them cool slightly. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges.
  9. For a traditional serving, you can roll up the lahmacun with fresh lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and a drizzle of tahini sauce or garlic sauce.

Bon appetit!
